Theoretical Framework and Rationale

Introduction

This website is designed to explore and present Near-Earth Object (NEO) data through interactive data visualisations. The aim is to provide users with an accessible way to understand the significance of NEOs, their proximity to Earth, and their potential hazards.

Rationale

The choice of data visualisation was driven by the need to display large amounts of data in a concise and engaging manner. The bubble chart was chosen to represent the relative size and various status of NEOs, as it allows for quick comparisons between different objects. The bar chart was chosen to highlight the closest approaching NEOs, providing users with a sense of scale and distance.

The aim was to make the data visualisations informative while also offering a somewhat engaging user experience, via tooltips and popup windows, in addition to an external reference to NASA's affiliate site should the user want to learn more about a particular NEO.

UI/UX

The interface was designed to be intuitive and user-friendly, with a focus on simplicity and accessibility. The use of simple and bold contrasting colours helps to distinguish between different visual elements and break up space.

The placement of the visualisations was chosen to give them prominence on the data page, encouraging user interaction while maintaining clarity. Furthermore, a clean and minimal design was used to avoid overwhelming users with information, providing clear labels, legends, and tooltips to guide the user.

Challenges and Solutions

Essay: Data Visualisation in Environmental Contexts

The following is the essay we had written this semester on data visualisation, and its application in a particular context.

Introduction

This marks the written piece for the semester, where we consider the effects of sound user design and coherent data visualisation in a piece of our choosing. I always try to orient my coursework around South Africa and the community around me, as it makes for a convenient way to both engage with schoolwork and explore topics related to the country, allowing me to become more knowledgeable.

Recently, I have developed an interest in plant and tree life, with my work last semester being based around the themes of deforestation and forestry in South Africa. I would like to continue with that focus here, and so the piece I have decided to work with is an interactive forestry map of South Africa, specifically in Kwa-Zulu Natal, as it has the densest segments of forestry in the country.

Data Visualisation Piece Overview

The map is taken from Global Forest Watch (GFW), an interactive platform that tracks growth, deforestation, and conservation efforts from around the globe, with data from South Africa as well. In addition, GFW uses satellite data and ground reporting to provide real-time information about tree cover loss, forest fires, and conservation efforts.

Visualisation Techniques and User Interface

The Global Forest Watch map is an excellent example of effective data visualisation. It utilises various visualisation techniques such as heat maps and satellite imagery overlays to communicate the extent of deforestation. The platform is also designed with user-centred principles in mind, ensuring ease of navigation and a clear, intuitive interface that allows both experts and the general public to engage with the data.

The user interface is clean, with easy-to-understand legends, interactive layers, and the ability to zoom in and out for finer or broader views of the forest cover. Users can also click on specific regions for more detailed data on deforestation rates, causes, and conservation efforts.

Impact and Engagement

One of the main strengths of the Global Forest Watch platform is its ability to engage users with real-time data. By providing clear and accessible visual representations of deforestation, it not only informs users but also encourages engagement with environmental issues. The inclusion of features such as email alerts for specific regions or types of forest cover loss makes the platform interactive and action-oriented.

In terms of impact, the platform’s data is widely used by environmental organisations, policymakers, and activists to monitor deforestation trends and advocate for better environmental policies. It has also become a valuable tool for educational purposes, raising awareness about the importance of forests and the threats they face.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Global Forest Watch serves as a powerful tool for visualising and understanding deforestation patterns. Its user-centric design, combined with sophisticated data visualisation techniques, makes it an effective platform for both professionals and the general public. By translating complex environmental data into accessible visuals, GFW helps raise awareness about deforestation and encourages users to take action to protect forests.
